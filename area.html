Wageningen & surroundings

Wageningen and
what surrounds it

Wageningen is a small university city with a genuinely international feel — Wageningen University (WUR) draws researchers and students from all over the world, which shows in the restaurants, the cafés, and the general atmosphere. It's compact and easy to get around.

Outside the centre, the pace drops off immediately. The Wageningse Berg — a hilly, forested ridge rare for the Netherlands — begins within walking distance of the park. The Rhine is 15 minutes by bike. The Veluwe is 20 minutes by car.

World-Class Cycling

The area has excellent cycling infrastructure — forest paths, Rhine dyke routes, and connections through Rhenen, Ede, and Arnhem. The terrain has more variety than most of the Netherlands. Bike hire is available locally.

Starting from your door
🌳

Wageningse Berg & Forest Walks

The park borders the Wageningse Berg — a hilly ridge covered in oak, beech, and heather. Footpaths start at the cottage gate. It's particularly good in autumn.

0 min walk
🌿

Belmonte Arboretum

Managed by Wageningen University, Belmonte Arboretum is a beautiful botanical garden home to rare and unusual trees from around the world. Breathtaking in spring blossom and autumn colour. About 10 minutes by car, or a pleasant cycle through the forest.

10 min drive
🛶

The Rhine & Grebbeberg

Follow the river Rhine westward for flat cycling and walking along the dykes. The Grebbeberg, nearby, is a place of natural beauty and historical depth — rolling wooded terrain with a poignant wartime past that still moves visitors deeply.

15 min drive
🏙️

Wageningen City Centre

A lively, international university town full of independent cafés, weekly markets, and excellent restaurants. The main street (Hoogstraat) has everything you need, and the market on Wednesday and Saturday mornings is excellent for local produce and Dutch cheese.

6 min drive · 10 min by bike
🦅

Wildlife & Birdwatching

Red kites, deer, foxes, and rare woodland birds are frequent visitors in and around the park. Bring binoculars — the early mornings are extraordinary. Deer are commonly seen at the forest edge at dawn and dusk.

Wednesday and Saturday morning markets on the main square. Fresh local produce, Dutch cheeses, flowers, and bread. A proper market worth setting your alarm for.
